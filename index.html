Meet Professor
Kundan K. Singh Sagar

Welcome to the K2S2 Group, where we explore the fascinating world of nanoclusters and catalysis. Our research focuses on synthesizing and characterizing novel inorganic and organometallic complexes, with a special emphasis on their applications in energy-relevant small molecule activation and innovative organic transformations.

Dr. Kundan K. Singh is an Assistant Professor of Chemistry at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Dharwad, India. His research interests lie in the synthesis and characterization of new inorganic and organometallic complexes, as well as metal nanoclusters. These compounds are useful for their potential in energy-relevant small molecule activation, innovative organic transformations, and mechanistic understanding.

Before joining IIT Dharwad, Dr. Kundan K. Singh expanded his research horizons through postdoctoral fellowships at prestigious institutions in the United States; Johns Hopkins University, the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, and Carnegie Mellon University. These experiences significantly broadened his research skills and approach, enhancing his understanding of various advanced methodologies in synthetic chemistry and catalysis. Dr. Kundan K. Singh completed his Ph.D. in 2017 from CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory (NCL), Pune, India, under the guidance of Prof. Sayam Sen Gupta. His research primarily focused on high-valent iron-oxo metal complexes. His early research interests were sparked during his time as a master's student at Banaras Hindu University (BHU), where he worked under the mentorship of Prof. D. S. Pandey. It was at BHU that his passion for chemistry and research first took shape. Hailing from Chikani village in East Champaran, Bihar, India, Dr. Kundan K. Singh completed his early education at Saraswati Shishu/Vidya Mandir in Raxaul, located just 7 km away from his village. This modest beginning in a rural area fueled his determination to excel in academia and make significant contributions to the scientific community. Dr. Singh's research is driven by a commitment to developing sustainable and innovative chemical processes that have far-reaching applications in energy and the environment. Through his academic and professional journey, he continues to inspire future generations of scientists and contribute to the growth of chemical research in India and beyond.
